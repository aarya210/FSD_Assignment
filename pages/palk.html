Welcome to Palakkad!

About

Palakkad, or Palghat, is a city in Kerala with no coastal line. The district opens the state to the rest of the country through the Palakkad

The 18th-century Palakkad Fort has sturdy battlements, a moat and a Hanuman temple on its grounds.

North, on the Kalpathy River, the 15th-century Viswanatha Swamy Temple is the main venue of the famous Ratholsavam chariot festival.

Northeast, near Malampuzha Dam, the town of Malampuzha has a rock garden created from recycled materials.

Places to Look out for

Deriving its name from the Malayalam words Pala (Alsteriascholaris) and Kadu (forest), this place was once a stunning expanse of forest covered with the sweet-scented flowers of the Pala tree. A potpourri of Tamil and Kerala culture, some of the finest Carnatic musicians hail from this district which continues to be a principally agrarian society
